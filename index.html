I Stopped Asking Permission to Build Wealth

Four years ago, I bet everything on creative finance and a community of people who think differently about deals. Here's what happened.

Chapter 01

Where It Started

In 2019, I was working finance sales in Beverly Hills. On paper, it looked good. In reality, I was spending three hours getting there and two and a half hours getting back—every single day.

The money had a ceiling. The company had problems. And I had this growing feeling that I was building someone else's dream on someone else's timeline.

I refused to let anyone else dictate what I was worth.

So at the end of 2019, I walked away. Not with a plan. Not with a safety net. Just with a conviction that I was done letting someone else decide my earning potential.

Chapter 02

The Lonely Road

Then 2020 happened. Pandemic. Lockdowns. The world shut down—and I went all in.

YouTube University. Every course I could find. Meetups over Zoom. I started wholesaling, grinding solo, trying to figure out real estate from scratch.

It took me nine to ten months to close my first deal.

Nine months of cold calls, dead ends, and learning the hard way that information alone doesn't make you dangerous.

Then came the JV deal I thought would change everything. Projected to net around $40,000. I was green, eager, and trusting.

I walked away with $5,000.

Not because the deal was bad. Because I didn't have the experience, the mentorship, or the network to protect what I'd built. I got taken advantage of—and I didn't even realize it until it was too late.

That loss taught me more than any course ever did.
Chapter 03

Then Everything Changed

I started studying creative finance—not just wholesaling, not just flipping, but the actual structure of deals. Terms. Seller financing. Subject-to. The mechanics that most people never learn because they're too busy chasing assignments.

I found a seller. Applied what I'd learned. Closed the deal. Then I JV'd with someone inside SubTo—a community I'd been watching from the outside—and made $14,000.

More than the money, it was the clarity. For the first time, I wasn't guessing. I had a framework. I had people around me who had done it before.

I joined SubTo in December 2021.
That was over four years ago.

Within a month or two, I'd earned back the cost of membership. Not through some magic formula—but through proximity to people who were already doing what I wanted to do.

Chapter 04

Creative Finance Isn't an Asset Class.
It's a Lens.

Here's what most people get wrong about creative finance.

They think it's a strategy. A technique. A hack for buying houses without banks.

It's not. It's a lens.

Creative finance is a way of looking at any asset—any deal—and asking: "What's the structure that makes this work for everyone?"

50%
Real Estate

The core. Subject-to, seller financing, wraps, creative acquisitions. This is where the biggest opportunities live and where most of the community is focused. It's the foundation.

20%
Business

Same lens, different asset. Structuring business acquisitions, partnerships, and deals using creative terms. The framework applies wherever value exchanges hands.

30%
Vehicles

My unique lane. I became known as "the SubTo car guy" — not because I love cars more than houses, but because I saw an entire market nobody was structuring deals in. Same method, untapped space.

Terms Over Price. That's the operating system I built from everything I've learned inside SubTo, applied across every asset class I touch.

But the method isn't the point. The thinking is.

Chapter 05

Content Is Everywhere.
Community Isn't.

You can learn creative finance on YouTube. Seriously. The information is out there.

So why would you pay to join a community?

Because information isn't the bottleneck. Proximity is.

Partners send me deals I never would have found on my own. Not from marketing—from relationships.

Conversations have saved me from six-figure mistakes. The kind of insight you can't get from a YouTube comment section.

Reputation compounds over time. The more people know your work, the more deals find you. That only happens inside a room.

Feedback loops accelerate everything. I learn from other people's wins and mistakes in real time—not on a 6-month delay.

You can't YouTube your way into someone's deal flow. You can't Google your way into a partnership. You can't watch your way into a reputation.

The people around you determine the deals in front of you. That's not a motivational quote—it's math.

Chapter 06

Everyone's Asking the Wrong Question About AI

I hear it constantly: "AI is replacing everything. How am I supposed to compete?"

Wrong question.

AI is a tool. A powerful one. It can analyze deals, write contracts, build marketing material, run systems—and it's only getting better.

But here's what AI cannot do:

×

Build trust with a seller sitting across from you at a kitchen table.

×

Shake hands with a partner and mean it.

×

Carry a reputation earned over years of doing right by people.

×

Replace the human nuance of a negotiation where both sides need to feel heard.

Here's the flip

Within a strong community, AI becomes exponentially more powerful.

Shared prompts. Shared workflows. Shared deal structures. Shared systems. One person figures out an AI workflow that saves 10 hours a week—and suddenly 5,000 people have it.

That's not something you get alone. That's a network effect. And that's exactly what's happening inside SubTo right now.
