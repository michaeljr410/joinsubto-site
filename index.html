My Story

How I Got Here

In 2019, I was working finance sales in Beverly Hills. On paper, it looked good. In reality, I was spending three hours getting there and two and a half hours getting back—every single day. The money had a ceiling. The company had problems. And I had this growing feeling that I was building someone else's dream on someone else's timeline.

I refused to let anyone else dictate what I was worth.

So at the end of 2019, I walked away. Then 2020 happened. Pandemic, lockdowns, the world shut down—and I went all in. YouTube University. Every course I could find. Meetups over Zoom. I started wholesaling solo, grinding from scratch.

It took me nine to ten months to close my first deal. Then came the JV deal I thought would change everything. Projected to net around $40,000. I was green, eager, and trusting. I walked away with $5,000. I got taken advantage of—and I didn't even realize it until it was too late.

That loss taught me more than any course ever did. I started studying creative finance—not just wholesaling, not just flipping, but the actual structure of deals. I found a seller, applied what I'd learned, and JV'd with someone inside SubTo. I made $14,000.

I joined SubTo in December 2021.
That was over four years ago.

Within a month or two, I'd earned back the cost of membership. Not through some magic formula—but through proximity to people who were already doing what I wanted to do.

Most people think creative finance is a strategy for buying houses without banks. It's not. It's a lens. A way of looking at any asset—any deal—and asking: "What's the structure that makes this work for everyone?"

20%

The AI Question

Everyone's Asking the Wrong Question About AI

I hear it constantly: "AI is replacing everything. How am I supposed to compete?"

Wrong question.

AI is a tool. A powerful one. It can analyze deals, write contracts, build marketing material, run systems—and it's only getting better. But here's what AI cannot do:

×

Build trust with a seller sitting across from you at a kitchen table.

×

Shake hands with a partner and mean it.

×

Carry a reputation earned over years of doing right by people.

×

Replace the human nuance of a negotiation where both sides need to feel heard.

Here's the flip

Within a strong community, AI becomes exponentially more powerful. Shared prompts. Shared workflows. Shared deal structures. One person figures out an AI workflow that saves 10 hours a week—and suddenly 5,000 people have it.

That's a network effect you can't get alone. And that's exactly what's happening inside SubTo right now.

Questions

Frequently Asked

Is SubTo only for real estate?

+
No. SubTo is a creative finance community. While real estate is the core—subject-to, seller financing, wraps, creative acquisitions—the framework applies far beyond houses. I personally apply the same principles to vehicles and business structures. Creative finance is a lens, not an asset class. You'll learn to think in terms, not just price, and that thinking applies everywhere.

Is this worth it if I'm brand new?

+
Honestly? That's when it's worth the most. I spent almost a year grinding solo before I joined—nine months to my first deal, and I still got taken advantage of on it. If I'd had this community from day one, I would have compressed that timeline dramatically. You don't need experience to start. You need proximity to people who have it.

What if I don't have the money right now?

+
I get it. When I joined, it wasn't an easy decision financially. The cost of staying stuck is higher than the cost of getting in the room. I earned my membership back within the first two months. That said, if you truly can't afford it, focus on getting your first deal—then use that momentum to invest in yourself. Don't go into debt for a community. But don't let fear disguise itself as logic either.

What if I'm worried about AI taking over?

+
Good—you're paying attention. But you're asking the wrong question. AI is a tool, not a replacement. It can't build trust, carry a reputation, or negotiate with nuance. What it CAN do is amplify everything you're already doing—especially inside a community. When one person builds an AI workflow, 5,000 people benefit. That's a network effect you can't get alone.

Do I need to be into cars to benefit?

+
Not at all. Vehicles are my lane—I became known as "the SubTo car guy" because I saw an untapped market. But the community is primarily focused on real estate. You don't need to touch a car deal unless you want to. The principles I teach apply to any asset. Find your lane.

How fast can I make my investment back?

+
It depends entirely on you. I earned mine back in about two months. Some people do it faster. Some take longer. There are no guarantees—anyone who promises one is lying. What I can tell you is that the community puts opportunities in front of you that you wouldn't find alone. If you apply yourself, the math tends to work out.

Can't I just learn this on YouTube for free?

You can learn a lot for free. I did—for almost a year. The information is out there. But here's what YouTube can't give you: deal flow, partnerships, accountability, real-time feedback, and a reputation inside a room full of active operators. Information isn't the bottleneck. Proximity is.

What is creative finance, exactly?

+
Creative finance is structuring deals using terms—not just price. Instead of going to a bank for a traditional loan, you negotiate directly with the seller or business owner to create a structure that works for both sides. Subject-to existing financing, seller financing, wraps, lease options—these are all creative finance tools. It's how you acquire assets with less capital, more flexibility, and better positioning.
