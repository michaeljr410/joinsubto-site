the framework

Creative finance isn't a strategy. It's a lens.

Most people hear "creative finance" and think it's a hack for buying houses without a bank. It's not. It's a way of looking at any asset — any deal — and asking: what's the structure that makes this work for everyone?

Think in terms. Not price. That's the shift.

The core is real estate — subject-to, seller financing, wraps, lease options. That's where the biggest opportunities are and where most of the community is focused. Houses, multi-family, commercial, buildings downtown. But here's what I realized after years inside: it was never about a house.

You can buy car washes. Laundromats. RV parks. Businesses. Boats. Planes. And vehicles — that became my lane. I became "the SubTo car guy" because I saw a market nobody was structuring deals in, and I started solving those problems at a very high level. You don't have to touch cars — I just did. But the opportunity was sitting right there.

The amount of different opportunities to make money in creative finance are absolutely insane. People inside SubTo are doing deals across every asset class you can think of. You can learn about taxes, business structures, entity formation — all of it inside this community. And I have one of the most unique stories because I came in wanting to understand real estate, and I did — but what also happened was just watching other problems get presented that creative finance could solve.
